CYCLONE

🌪WHAT IS A CYCLONE?

• A cyclone is a powerful, rotating storm system with strong winds and heavy rain
• It forms over warm ocean waters and moves toward the land
• Can cause floods, destruction of property, and loss of life
• Affects coastal areas, villages, cities, and transportation

⚡CAUSES OF CYCLONES

• Warm ocean water (usually 26°C or more)
• High humidity and low-pressure areas over the sea
• Rapid upward movement of warm, moist air
• Earth’s rotation helps spin the storm (Coriolis effect)
• Climate change increasing sea temperatures

⚠ Warning Signs of an Approaching Cyclone 

• Sudden dark clouds and heavy winds
• Rapid drop in air pressure
• High waves and storm surges at the coast
• Continuous, heavy rainfall
• Alerts and warnings from meteorological departments

🚫 Effects of Cyclones

• Flooding of homes and roads
• Destruction of houses, trees, power lines
• Loss of crops, livestock, and income
• Water contamination and disease outbreaks
• Disruption of transport, electricity, and communication
• Displacement of people and rescue operations

🛡 Preparedness Tips

• Build cyclone shelters in coastal areas
• Strengthen houses and infrastructure
• Keep emergency kits ready
• Educate communities on cyclone safety drills
• Monitor weather updates from trusted sources
• Plan a meeting point with family in case you get separated.
• Charge Devices like mobile phones and power banks.

🧷WHAT TO DO DURING A CYCLONE? 

• Stay indoors and away from windows
• Keep emergency lights, food, and water ready
• Turn off electricity and gas connections
• Listen to the radio/TV for official updates
• Evacuate if told to – don’t wait until it’s too late
• Stay in a strong room or cyclone shelter if available

🧠 What If…? Cyclone Scenarios

🏠 If Your Village Is Hit by a Cyclone and You’re in a Shelter 

What to Do:
• Stay calm and follow instructions from authorities
• Help elderly, children, and injured people
• Share water and food with others
• Avoid spreading rumors – follow official news only
• Keep IDs and important papers safe and dry

🚑 If Someone Is Injured or Trapped 

What to Do:
• Call emergency helplines or nearby rescue teams
• Don’t attempt risky rescues alone
• Provide basic first aid if trained
• Use clean cloth to stop bleeding
• Keep the person warm and hydrated

🧍‍♀️ If You Want to Help After a Cyclone 

What to Do:
• Volunteer in relief work (only with authorized groups)
• Help clean up local areas safely
• Support food and clothing distribution
• Assist in spreading information on aid and support
• Provide emotional support to those affected

🦺EMERGENCY KIT CHECKLIST

• Battery torch and extra batteries
• Bottled water (3L per person/day)
• Non-perishable food
• First aid kit with basic medicines
• Power bank for phone
• Whistle (for rescue signal)
• Important documents in waterproof pouch
• Blanket and spare clothes
• Candles and Matches in a waterproof container
• Chlorine tablets or powdered water purifier

💣Some Tips For College Students

• Store offline maps of safe shelters and high-ground routes • Fill water bottles early; hostel tanks can get contaminated after a cyclone • Use SMS or low-data apps when networks are weak during storms
