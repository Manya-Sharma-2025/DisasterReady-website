THUNDERSTORM

🧠 What is a Thunderstorm?

• A thunderstorm is a weather event with thunder, lightning, heavy rain, and strong winds.
• It can cause power cuts, falling trees, flash floods, and injuries if safety measures aren’t followed.

🚨 Common Causes & Triggers

• Intense heating of the Earth's surface → warm air rises → cloud formation.
• Moisture & unstable air → leads to heavy rains
• Low-pressure systems bring strong winds and lightning.
• Climate change can make storms stronger and more frequent.

🛑 Prevention & Preparedness Tip

• Always check weather forecasts before going out.
• Charge phones & power banks before predicted storms.
• Keep important documents safe in waterproof bags.
• Trim weak tree branches near homes and hostels
• Avoid scheduling outdoor events when storm warnings are issued.
• Have a basic emergency kit ready (see checklist below).

🌩️ Scenarios

✅ If You're Indoors

• Stay inside – avoid balconies, terraces, and open windows.
• Unplug electrical appliances to avoid damage from power surges
• Stay away from doors, windows, and metallic objects.
• Use battery-powered lights instead of candles.
• Avoid using wired phones and running water (lightning can travel through pipes).

🚫 If You're Outdoors

• Find immediate shelter in a safe building.
• Stay away from trees, poles, and metal objects.
• Do not stand near water.
• Squat low, feet together, hands over ears, and make yourself small.
• Don’t lie flat on the ground — it increases lightning risk.

🌩️If someone is struck by lightning:

• Call 108 / 102 immediately.
• Check breathing & heartbeat. Start CPR if needed

🔐 If Power Goes Out

• Use flashlights, not candles (to avoid fire hazards).
• Keep your phone on low power mode and avoid unnecessary usage.
• Turn off heavy appliances — sudden power return can damage them.

⚡ Lightning Safety 

• Inside If you hear thunder, lightning is close enough to strike you — seek shelter immediately.
• a building or car? Stay there until 30 minutes after the last thunderclap.

🌊 Flash Floods During Storms

• Avoid basements or low-lying areas — they can fill with water quickly.
• Do not walk or drive through flooded streets; just 6 inches of moving water can knock you down.
• Stay tuned to local alerts on FM, news apps, or weather notifications.

🎒 Emergency Kit Checklist

• pouch for documents
• Emergency contacts list
• Raincoat / umbrella
• Small towel & face mask
• Torch / flashlight + extra batteries 
• Fully charged power bank
• First-aid kit 🩹
• Bottled drinking water
• Non-perishable snacks
• Whistle for signaling help.

🎓 Extra Tips for College Students

• Always share your location with family or friends if stuck outside.
• Keep offline maps downloaded on your phone.
• Never ignore IMD thunderstorm alerts.
