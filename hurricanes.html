🌊 What are Hurricanes? 

• Powerful tropical storms over warm oceans
• Known as cyclones / typhoons in other regions
• Wind speed: ≥ 74 mph (119 km/h)
• Caused by warm moist air + Earth’s rotation

🌪 Other Names of Hurricanes 

• Hurricane 🌊 → North Atlantic Ocean & eastern/central North Pacific ocean
• Typhoon 🌏 → Northwest Pacific (near Asia)
• Cyclone 🌐 → westernSouth Pacific & Indian Ocean
• Severe Cyclonic Storm 🌩 → Bay of Bengal & Arabian Sea (India region)

⚡ Parts of a Hurricane 

• Calm Eye → in the center
• Eye Wall → Strongest winds, heavy rain
• Rain Bands → Spirals of clouds bringing storms its sie.

🚨 Dangers of Hurricanes 

• 🌬 Extreme winds → damage to homes, trees, power lines
• 🌊 Flooding → water covers towns& roads
• 🌐 Storm surge → sea water rushes onto land
• 🏔 Landslides → caused by heavy rain in hilly areas

🌊 Causes of Hurricanes 

• Warm Ocean Water
          o Temperature of sea surface above 26°C (79°F) provides energy.
• Moist Air
	  o Warm, humid air rises from ocean → creates thunderstorms.
• Low Air Pressure
	  o Rising air reduces surface pressure → draws in more air.
• Earth’s Rotation (Coriolis Effect)
	  o  Makes the storm system spin and form a cyclone.
• Favorable Wind Conditions
          o Weak vertical wind shear (no strong winds breaking the system apart).

✅ Before the Hurricane. 

• Aware people about hurricanes.
• Follow weather updates & official warnings.
• Pack emergency kit.
• store drinking water
• charge phones 
• board windows 
• keep important documents safe.
• plan evacuation routes.
• keep local map.

✅ During the Hurricane. 

• Stay in safe room/basement.
• Away from windows.
• Use flashlight/torches (not candles).
• Listen to radio/alerts.
• Avoid using electrical appliances

✅ After the Hurricane. 

• Storm is over but damage everywhere.
• Wait for official “all clear” message
• Avoid broken power lines and damaged structures.
• Help injured people.

🌀 Hurricane Emergency Kit Essentials 🌀 

 
1. 💧 Drinking water – at least 3 days supply
2. 🍲 Non-perishable food – canned or packaged items
3. 💊 Medicines & first aid kit – prescription meds, bandages, antiseptic
4. 🔦 Flashlight & extra batteries
5. 📱 Charged phone & power bank
6. 📝 Important documents – ID, insurance papers, medical records
7. 🧥 Clothes & blankets – for warmth & comfort
8. 🧹 Basic tools – knife, rope, whistle, duct tape
9. 🧴 Hygiene items – soap, sanitizer, toilet paper, feminine products, garbage bags.

• 🚨 All in one Emergency Number – 112

• 🔥 Fire Brigade – 101

🎓 Extra Tips for College Students 

• 👥 Buddy System – Stay with roommates or friends; avoid being alone during evacuation.
• 🧠 Mental Health – Hurricanes can be stressful. Stay calm, help peers, and support each other.
• 🏫 Know Campus Emergency Plans – Learn your college’s evacuation routes, safe shelters, and alert systems.
