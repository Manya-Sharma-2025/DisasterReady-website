Tsunami


🧠 What is a Tsunami?

A tsunami is a big, powerful wave in the ocean that is usually caused by an earthquake, volcano, or landslide under the sea.
When it reaches land, it can flood areas and cause a lot of damage.

🔍 Causes of Tsunamis

  • Underwater earthquakes (most common cause) 🌎
    • 

  • Volcanic eruptions beneath the ocean 🌋
    • 

  • Landslides falling into the sea 🪨
    • 

  • Glacial collapse or rapid ice melting ❄️
    • 

  • Meteorite impacts hitting the ocean ☄️

    • ⚠️ Warning Signs of a Tsunami

  • Sudden strong earthquake near the coast.
    • 

  • Ocean water rapidly receding or the shoreline suddenly drying up.
    • 

  • Loud roaring or rumbling sounds from the sea.
    • 

  • Official tsunami warnings from IMD or disaster authorities.
    •

    🛑 Preparedness Tips

  • Know if your area is a tsunami-prone zone.
    • 

  • Keep a ready-to-grab emergency kit (see checklist below).
    • 

  • Identify safe evacuation routes to higher ground.
    • 

  • Stay informed via IMD alerts and local weather reports
    • .

  • Practice drills at school, hostel, or home to prepare for quick evacuation.
    •

    🏃 What to Do During a Tsunami

    ✅ If You’re on the Coast

  • Move immediately to higher ground – at least 30 meters above sea level if possible.
    • 

  • Do not wait for official instructions if you feel a strong earthquake — act fast.
    • 

  • Stay away from beaches, harbors, and riverbanks.
    •

    ✅ If You’re Indoors

  • Grab your emergency kit and evacuate calmly.
    • 

  • Turn off electricity, gas, and water if there’s time.
    • 

  • Avoid using elevators — use stairs instead.
    •

    🚗 If You’re Driving

  • Drive inland and away from the coastline.
    • 

  • Do not park near bridges, rivers, or beaches.
    • 

  • Follow evacuation signs and traffic instructions.
    •

    🌊 After the Tsunami

  • Stay in safe areas until authorities announce “All Clear”.
    • 

  • Avoid walking in flooded areas — water may be contaminated.
    • 

  • Do not return home until officials confirm it’s safe.
    • 

  • Be careful of debris, broken glass, and fallen wires.
    • 

  • Help others if safe, especially children, elderly, and injured.
    •

    🎒 Emergency Kit Checklist

  • Torch / flashlight + spare batteries 🔦
    • 

  • Fully charged power bank
    • 

  • First-aid kit 🩹
    • 

  • Bottled drinking water (at least 3L per person)
    • 

  • Non-perishable food (biscuits, dry fruits, etc.) 🍪
    • 

  • Whistle for signaling help 📢
    • 

  • Waterproof pouch for documents 📄
    • 

  • Extra clothes + raincoat
    •

  • Always keep your phone charged during weather alerts.
    • 

  • Learn your campus’s tsunami evacuation plan.
    • 

  • Store offline maps of safe zones.
    • 

  • Travel light — prioritize safety, not belongings.
