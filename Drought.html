DROUGHTS

🧠 What is a Drought? 

• A drought happens when there is not enough rainfall for a long time
• Causes dry land, water shortage, crop failure, and even food crisis
• Can affect villages, cities, farmers, animals, and drinking water supply

🔍 Causes of Droughts 

• Very low or no rainfall over months or years
• Deforestation (fewer trees = less rainfall)
• Overuse of groundwater and rivers
• Climate change and rising temperatures
• Poor water management (no storage, wastage)

⚠️ Signs of a Drought 

• Rivers, lakes, and wells dry up
• Soil becomes dry and cracked
• Crops start failing
• Water supply gets restricted
• Hot, dusty winds and no clouds

🚫 Effects of Droughts

• Lack of drinking water
• Shortage of food and water for animals
• Farmers lose crops and income
• Migration from villages to cities
• Higher prices of food and water
• Increased risk of wildfires

🛑 Prevention & Preparedness

• Save water daily – don’t waste
• Use buckets instead of showers
• Fix leaking taps and pipes
• Plant more trees – trees help bring rain
• Build rainwater harvesting systems
• Educate communities on water conservation
• Use drip irrigation in farming
• Store water in tanks or underground reservoirs

💧 What to Do During a Drought

• Use water only for essentials (drinking, cooking)
• Reuse water when possible (e.g., from washing vegetables for plants)
• Drink clean, filtered or boiled water
• Stay indoors during extreme heat
• Avoid burning waste – increases heat and dryness

What If…? Drought Scenarios

🌾 If you’re a Student in a Village & Your Family’s Crops Have Failed

• Help spread awareness on government drought relief programs
• Connect with the nearest agriculture officer or Krishi Vigyan Kendra
• Learn about drought-resistant crops or drip irrigation
• Collect info on crop insurance or compensation
• Support the family emotionally and encourage education continuation

🏥 If someone Gets Dehydrated or Suffers Heatstroke 

Signs: Dizziness, dry mouth, headache, no sweating, confusion
• Move the person to a cool, shaded area
• Give small sips of clean water or ORS
• Place a wet cloth on their forehead and neck
• Call a health center or dial 102 / 108 ambulance immediately

🌿 If you Want to Help Your Community During a Drought 

• Organize a tree planting drive or awareness rally
• Help elders fill online forms for government water schemes
• Distribute simple tips for water saving door-to-door
• Distribute food, water and medicines.

🧃 Emergency Kit for Drought Conditions 

• Bottled water (at least 3L per person/day)
• Water purification tablets or filters
• Oral rehydration salts (ORS)
• Non-perishable food (dry snacks, biscuits, etc.)
• Wet wipes (for hygiene when water is limited)
• Torch and batteries
• Copies of IDs

📞 Emergency Support 

• Local water board or municipal helpline
• Health department in case of heat stroke or dehydration
• NGOs or government drought relief helplines
• School or college support services
• Local farm support or Krishi Vigyan Kendras

🎓 Extra Tips for College & Rural Students 

• Encourage community water-saving programs
• Organize awareness drives in schools/villages
• Volunteer with NGOs during drought relief
• Take part in tree planting campaigns
• Learn about climate change adaptation techniques
