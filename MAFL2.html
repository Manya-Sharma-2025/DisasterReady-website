FLOODS

What is a Flood?

A flood occurs when water overflows from rivers, lakes, or seas onto normally dry land.
It can result from heavy rainfall, dam failure, melting glaciers, or cyclones, and can cause property damage, water contamination, injuries, and even fatalities.

🔍 Causes of Floods

  • Heavy & continuous rainfall 🌧️
    • 

  • River overflow due to excess water release 🌊
    • 

  • Dam failure or breaches 🏞️
    • 

  • Cyclones & storm surges 🌀
    • 

  • Glacier melting in mountain regions ❄️
    • 

  • Poor drainage and urban planning 🏘️
    • 

  • Encroachment on riverbanks & wetlands 🏗
    •

    ⚠️ Warning Signs of Floods

  • Sudden rise in river or lake water levels.
    • 

  • Heavy rainfall alerts from the Indian Meteorological Department (IMD).
    • 

  • Waterlogging and overflowing drains.
    • 

  • Rumbling sounds from distant water movement.
    • 

  • SMS or app notifications from NDMA / local disaster authorities.

    • Planning for floods:

  • Purchase flood insurance for homeowners or renters .
    • 

  • See map to determine if you lie in area prone to flood risks . If yes, try planning your escape routes.
    • 

  • Report clogged catch basins. 
    • 

  • Have your important documents uploaded to digilocker .
    •


    Preparing for floods:
    1. Stay informed: 
  • Stay alert for any updates , warnings, evacuation routes and emergency shelter locations.
    • 

  • Sign up for local alerts (radio , sms and apps like NDMA/IMD).
    • 

  • Know your areas flood risk zones.
    • 

  • Check if officials are planning to construct barriers to avoid flood in your area. 
    •
    2. Make an emergency kit: 
  • Lots of drinking water bottle.
    • 

  • Packed food items.
    •  

  • First aid kit , essential medicines, ORS.
    • 

  • Torchlight , extra batteries . 
    • 

  • Power bank+ charged phone. 
    • 

  • Plastic bags (to protect documents and electronics , enough money) only , if able to carry them .
    •
    3. Home safety : 
  • Keep important documents in waterproof pouches.
    • 

  • Store essential valuables in higher shelves.
    • 

  • Switch the electrical switch and main gas valve ofF.
    • 

  • Move the stove and stored food essentials to higher places.
    • 

  • Install “check valves” to avoid backflow of drainage due to flood.
    • 

  • If possible you can also have flood safety barriers in front of your houses .
    Sandbags can be efficient too.
    •
    4. Family plan: 
  • Decide a safe meeting spot if separated could be emergency shelter or higher ground area. Remember the route.
    • 

  • Memorize the emergency contacts (can involve out of state relative numbers and address too , so they can help to communicate and more)
    • 

  • Teach kids what to do(climb ,higher ground , never walk in fast water)
    •
    If you have time to evacuate (hours/ days before): 
  • Pack your belonging ,  documents and money .
    • 

  • Keep your car ready , with full fuel and emergency kit.
    • 

  • Move with your family or pets to a designated shelter , relative’s house in safe zone or other higher ground.
    • 

  • Inform neighbours if possible.
    •
    Limited time: 
  • Move to nearby safe rooftops of strong buildings if evacuation to a shelter is not possible. 
    • 

  • Stay alert and visible – signal rescuers if needed .
    •
    /



    SCENARIOS :
    ✅ If You’re Indoors:     
  • Turn off electricity and gas supply to prevent shocks and fire hazards.
    • 

  • Move to higher floors if water starts entering your home/hostel.
    • 

  • Keep emergency lights, water, and dry snacks within reach.
    • 

  • Avoid drinking tap water — it may be contaminated.
    • 🚶 If You’re Outdoors : 
  • Move to higher ground immediately.Stay away from drains, manholes, and open sewage channels.
    • 

  • Do not walk through flowing water — just 6 inches of moving water can knock you down.
    • 

  • Avoid going near bridges, rivers, and dams.
    •
    🚗 If You’re Driving: 

  • Avoid driving through flooded roads — water can hide open manholes.
    • 

  • If the water level rises, abandon your vehicle and move to safety.
    • 

  • Follow official evacuation routes and instructions.
    •


    AFTER
    1. Safety first: 
  • Return home only when authorities say it is safe.
    • 

  • Watch out for weakened walls , damaged roads and open manholes.
    • 

  • Avoid walking or playing in standing water- it may hide hide sharp objects, debris or open drains.
    •
    2. Electricity and gas: 
  • Do not witch on electricity or gas until professionals declare it safe.
    • 

  • Get all wiring , appliancesand gas lines checked before use.
    •
    3. Clean up and hygiene 
  • Wear gloves and boots while cleaning mud and debris.
    • 

  • Disinfect your house with safe cleaning solutions.
    • 

  • Dispose of food items that were in floodwater.
    •
    4. Community support 
  • Check on neighbours especially elders and children.
    • 

  • Report damaged power lines or dangerous structures.
    • 

  • Keep updated with official news about relief , aid and medical camps.
    •
    5. Health precautions 
  • Boil or purify drinking water- floodwater is usually contaminated.
    • 

  • Wash hands and disinfect utensils before cooking or eating.
    • 

  • Beware of snakes , insects and animals that may have entered flooded areas.
    •
    6. Insurance 

  • If you have an insurance then document all the damages that you can get repaired through it . 
    • 

  • Also look out for government provided aid.
