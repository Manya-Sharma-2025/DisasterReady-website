BUILDING FIRES

🧠 What is a Building Fire? 

• A fire that starts inside a building (home, school, hostel, office, etc.)
• Spreads quickly and can cause serious injury or damage
• Needs quick action, calm thinking, and proper safety awareness

🚨 Common Causes 

• Unattended cooking
• Faulty or old electrical wiring
• Overloaded plug points or extension cords
• Candles, incense sticks, oil lamps near flammable items
• Matchsticks or lighters used carelessly
• Heaters or stoves placed too close to curtains or papers

🛑 Prevention Tips

• Never leave stoves or heaters running alone
• Switch off all appliances after use
• Avoid overloading plug sockets
• Place candles/incense safely, away from flammables
• Report faulty wiring or sparks immediately
• Use battery lights during power cuts – not candles
• Never block fire exits or staircases

🏬 SCENARIOS

🚒 If A Fire Starts 

• Shout “FIRE!” to alert everyone
• Exit the building immediately – don’t stop to collect things
• Crawl low under smoke – the air is clearer below
• Use stairs, never elevators
• If clothes catch fire: STOP, DROP & ROLL
• Call the fire emergency number: 101

🔐 What If You’re Stuck Inside And Can't Open The Door? 

• Check door with the back of your hand – if hot, don’t open
• Block smoke by placing a wet cloth at the door bottom
• Signal for help from a window using cloth or flashlight
• Call 101 and give your exact location

🌫️ Room is Filling with Smoke? 

• Crawl low – smoke rises, fresh air stays low
• Cover your mouth and nose with a wet cloth or mask
• Try to reach a balcony or open window
• Make noise, shout, use whistle – don’t hide

🎒 Emergency Kit – What to Include 

• Torch / flashlight
• Whistle
• First-aid kit
• Drinking water
• Power bank
• Emergency contact list
• Light snack / glucose
• Small towel or cloth mask
• Backup pendrive with digital copies of important files (IDs, projects, certificates)

🧪 Fire Drill & Safety Practice 

• Know 2 exits from every room
• Practice fire escape drills at school and college
• Decide a common meeting point outside
• Keep fire extinguishers and alarms working and accessible
• Learn to use a fire extinguisher (PASS method – Pull, Aim, Squeeze, Sweep)

🎓 Extra Safety Tips for College Students 

• Avoid using high-wattage appliances in hostels
• Use safe lighting options during outages
