🌍EARTHQUAKE

🧠 What is an Earthquake? 

  • An earthquake is the sudden shaking of the Earth's surface caused by movements of tectonic plates beneath the crust.
    • 

  • It can lead to building collapses, fires, landslides, tsunamis, and injuries, making quick and calm action essential.
    •

    🔍 Causes of Earthquakes

  • Tectonic plate movements → major cause 🌎
    • 

  • Volcanic eruptions 🌋
    • 

  • Underground explosions or mining activities ⛏️
    • 

  • Reservoir-induced seimicity (due to large dams)
    • 

  • Sudden fault slips beneath the Earth's surface.
    •

    ⚠️ Warning Signs of an Earthquake 

  • Unlike other disasters, earthquakes usually have no warning.
    • 

  • However, you might notice:
  1.Sudden ground shaking or rumbling sounds.
  2.Animals behaving unusually (running, barking, panicking).
  3.Official alerts via NDMA, IMD, or local authorities.
    •

    🛑 Preparedness Tips 

  • Know the safe spots in your house, hostel, or classroom (under sturdy tables or near interior walls).
    • 

  • Keep an emergency kit ready at all times (see checklist below).
    • 

  • Practice earthquake drills at school or college.
    • 

  • Secure bookshelves, heavy furniture, and hanging objects.
    • 

  • Store important documents in waterproof and fireproof pouches.
    • 

  • Stay informed about NDMA guidelines for earthquake-prone areas.
    •

    What to Do During an Earthquake
    ✅ If You’re Indoors 
    1. DROP, COVER & HOLD:
    

  • Drop to the ground.
    • 

  • Take cover under a sturdy desk or table.
    • 

  • Hold on until the shaking stops.
    • 

  • Stay away from windows, mirrors, and glass doors.
    • 

  • Avoid standing near bookshelves, cupboards, or hanging lights.
    • 

  • Do not use elevators — use stairs if evacuation is needed.
    •
    🚶 If You’re Outdoors: 
  • Move to an open area away from buildings, trees, poles, and wires.
    • 

  • Stay away from bridges, flyovers, and riverbanks.
    • 

  • Crouch low and protect your head and neck with your arms.
    •
    🚗 If You’re Driving 
  • Stop your vehicle in a clear, safe area.
    • 

  • Avoid parking under trees, bridges, and power lines.
    • 

  • Stay inside the vehicle until the shaking stops.
    •

    🏚️ After the Earthquake 
  • Be ready for aftershocks — move to open spaces if possible.
    • 

  • Check yourself and others for injuries — provide first aid if trained.
    • 

  • Turn off electricity, gas, and water supply if safe to do so.
    • 

  • Avoid using candles or open flames until you’re sure there’s no gas leak.
    • 

  • Listen to official updates via TV, radio, or mobile alerts.
    • 

  • Help children, elderly, and injured people reach safety.
    •
    First Aid For Injuries 
  • For cuts and bruises: Clean with antiseptic, cover with a sterile bandage.
    • 

  • For fractures: Immobilize the affected part and avoid unnecessary movement.
    • 

  • For unconscious individuals: Check breathing and call 108 immediately.
    • 

  • Provide ORS or water if someone shows signs of shock or dehydration. 
    •
    🎓 Extra Tips for College Students. 
  • Learn your college’s evacuation plan and nearest open grounds. 
    • 

  • Participate in mock drills for earthquake preparedness. 
    • 

  • Keep your ID card, wallet,  and phone within reach at all times. 
    • 

  • Avoid using stairs or elevators during heavy shaking. 
    • 

  • Stay calm and help classmates evacuate safely.
