ELECTRICAL FIRE🔥

🧠 What is an Electrical Fire?

•An electrical fire occurs when wiring, appliances, or electrical devices overheat, spark, or short-circuit, causing flames.
•These fires spread quickly and can cause serious damage, injuries, and even death if not handled safely.

🔍Common Causes of Electrical Fires

  • Overloaded plug points or extension cords 🔌
    • 

  • Faulty or old wiring⚡
    • 

  • Using damaged chargers, plugs, or cables 🔋
    • 

  • High-wattage appliances on low-capacity sockets 🛠️
    • 

  • Placing heaters, irons, or lamps near flammable items 🪵
    • 

  • Poor maintenance of switches, MCBs, and fuse boxes 🛠️

    • ⚠️ Warning Signs of Electrical Fire Risks 

  • Frequent sparks or burning smells from sockets.🔌
    • 

  • Flickering or dimming lights when appliances are used.💡
    • 

  • Hot or discolored switchboards.
    • 

  • Repeated tripping of MCBs or blown fuses.💥
    • 

  • Unusual buzzing or crackling sounds from wires.
    •

    🛑 Prevention Tips

  • Never overload plug points or extension cords.
    • 

  • Switch off appliances when not in use.
    • 

  • Replace damaged or loose wires immediately.
    • 

  • Avoid running wires under carpets — can cause overheating.
    • 

  • Use good-quality and certified electrical equipments.
    • 

  • Get wiring checked by an electrician regularly.👩‍🔧
    • 

  • Keep flammable items away from heaters, stoves, and lights.
    •


    ⚡Scenarios
    🚒 If an Electrical Fire starts:
    ✅ 1. Cut Off Power Supply 
  • Turn off the main switch if it’s safe to do so.
    • 

  • Unplug burning appliances if possible — but don’t touch wires with wet hands.
    •
    ✅ 2. Use the Right Fire Extinguisher🧯 
  • Never use water on an electrical fire ⚠️
    • 

  • Use a Class C or CO₂ extinguisher designed for electrical fires.
    • 

  • If you don’t have one, throw sand to smother small flames.
    •
    ✅ 3. Evacuate Safely
  • Shout “FIRE!” to alert everyone 📢
    • 

  • Leave the room and close doors behind you to slow fire spread🚪
    • 

  • Use stairs, never elevators.

    •
    ✅ 4. Call Emergency Services 📞
  • Dial 101 (Fire🚒) or 108 (Ambulance 🚑) immediately.
    • 

  • Give your exact location and details of the fire.
    • 🔐 If You’re Stuck Inside
  • Stay low to the ground — smoke rises, fresh air stays below.
    • 

  • Block gaps under doors with wet towels to stop smoke.
    • 

  • Signal for help using a torch, whistle, or waving cloth.
    • 

  • Move towards a balcony or open window if possible.
    •


    🩹 First Aid for Electrical Burns 
  • Switch off the power source before touching the person.
    • 

  • Cool burns with clean running water (not ice).
    • 

  • Cover wounds with a clean, dry cloth — do not apply ointments.
    • 

  • Call an ambulance (102 / 108) immediately.
    •
    🎓 Extra Tips for College Students 
    

  • Do not use high-wattage appliances in hostels (heaters, irons, hairdryers).
    • 

  • Report sparks or faulty wiring to authorities immediately.
    • 

  • Keep water bottles away from switchboards to prevent shocks.
    • 

  • Get wiring checked by an electrician regularly, especially in hostels.
    • 

  • Learn to operate a fire extinguisher (PASS method: Pull, Aim, Squeeze, Sweep).
    • 

  • Participate in drills.
